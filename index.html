## Atmospheric Reality Gap

Your homepage hero rotates between two slides: a Tour of Homes event banner and a member testimonial from Olympia Federal Savings. Neither one answers the question every first-time visitor asks in under three seconds — *what does this organization actually build or remodel?* The copy says "Building Strong Communities, One Home at a Time," but the hero delivers event promotion and a bank logo. That gap is not a design preference; it is a trust failure. A prospective member comparing associations decides within that three-second window whether this organization represents the quality tier they want to be associated with. Right now, the visual answer is: *we run events.*

The three member ads loaded above the fold — Family Man Remodeling's Tour of Homes banner, JER Remodeling's stock-photo jump, and the OMB house-ad asking "Why isn't your ad right here?" — mean a first-time visitor's initial visual experience is three competing commercial messages before they encounter a single piece of OMB's own identity. Your members' advertising is outranking your brand on your own homepage.

The trust cost: a prospective member or homeowner hiring a pro sees a site that looks like an ad network, not a regional authority. They click to a member's site and never come back.

## Visual Narrative Coherence

The filename `Unknown.png` — uploaded September 2025 and used twice as the Family Man Remodeling Tour of Homes ad — signals to any buyer or prospective member who inspects your page that assets are being dropped into the site without review. A file named `Unknown.png` is the digital equivalent of a placeholder that shipped to production. It appears at positions two and seven in your image stack, meaning it loads near the top of the page on both desktop and mobile.

Your header image pulls from `New_green_OMB_Header.jpg`, a file dated May 2018 — seven years old — sitting alongside a 2026 event calendar. The visual era of your branding and the operational year of your programming are completely misaligned. A high-value contractor or developer evaluating membership sees a 2026 Golf Classic promoted under what is effectively a 2018 banner. That contradiction signals an organization that updates its calendar but not its credibility markers.

The fracture cost: vendors and prospective members read visual inconsistency as organizational inconsistency. One competitor who looks current wins the inquiry you never received.

---

## Metadata and Conversion Gaps

Fourteen of your 16 content images carry no alt text — an 88% failure rate. This is not an accessibility footnote; it is 14 images that contribute zero signal to Google's understanding of what your pages contain. Your page title is 30 characters: "Home - Olympia Master Builders." Your meta description is missing entirely. Your og:image is missing, which means every share of omb.org on LinkedIn, Facebook, or in a text message renders with no preview image — a blank card next to every competitor who bothered to set one.

You rank #1 organically for "Olympia Master Builders," meaning people who already know your name find you. That ranking tells you nothing about whether you appear for "home builders association Olympia WA" or "find a contractor Thurston County" — the searches a homeowner or prospective member runs before they know your name. With no meta description and no og:image, even your branded #1 position produces a search snippet that cannot compete on click-through against a result with a populated description.

The forfeit: 14 unnamed images, zero social preview, and a truncated title are collectively suppressing both organic reach and the conversion rate of every share your members generate on your behalf.
